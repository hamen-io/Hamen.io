2.1: Determining Average Rate of Change

The average rate of change—abbreviated to, $$AROC$$—, is the average speed whereat a function changes over a specified interval of time or other independent variable.

Determining the average rate of change can be done by calculating the slope of a secant line—a secant line is simply a linear line drawn between point $$(0,0)$$, and point $$(0, 0)$$—

info

Though the average rate of change (AROC) may seem slightly similar to the slope, it's not; AROC measures overall change over an interval, while slope describes the steepness of a line.

For example, given the function $$f(x)=6x^2-3x+5$$ (which is graphed below), if you wanted to find the average rate of change, you could do so

Find the average rate of change—by determining the slope of the secant line— for the function: $$f(x)=6x^2-3x+5$$, between $$x=-5$$ and $$x=-1$$

We can find the slope of the secant by:

  1. Finding point $$a$$, and $$b$$, of the secant line; in our case, the two points are $$(-5, 3)$$, and $$(-1, -5)$$
  2. With these points, we can now input them into our original formula to find $$m_{sec}$$:
    $$m_{sec}=\frac{f(x_2)-f(x_1)}{x_2-x_1}$$ $$=\frac{f(-5)-f(3)}{(-5)-3}$$ $$=\frac{120}{-8}$$ $$=15$$

∴ the average rate of change is $$15 \frac{m}{s}$$
